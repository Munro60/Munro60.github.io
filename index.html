My Future Project Idea!

Introduction

The project in mind has lots of financial and technological requirements to function as intended . But, if done correctly I believe it could be a boon to the Fintech and trade sectors of the financial markets . There is a niche for many technology boosted financial aides and tools that has been capitalized on by many huge companies and banking conglomerates ranging from Westpac investing and Commsec (Commonwealth bank) all the way to international market watchers and tool providers such as Bloomberg. My project hopes to unify the past historical data of not just companies, firms and agencies, but also individual businesspeople. I hope to be able to develop a platform for comparing successful businesspeople movements from company to company and how the moves affected the environments they moved into. To in theory be able to track the movements of positive return individuals, to hypothesis potential investment increases. But with simple presentation of a user interface to make it easy to follow for people who don’t understand the background complexities of the numerical data.

Why do I want to do this?

Well , when I first started day trading as a hobby , the complexities of all the terminology and numerical data seemed very off putting when you’re looking at vast spreadsheets of data that could either be telling you its going to be a successful sure-thing or a near impossible financial recovery .That is part of the gamble that is stock trading .However ,there are many tools and services available that have been developed by firms and banks to make trading more approachable or simplified to newcomers . Most are quite expensive , charging subscription fees or taking commission off trades to make a profit. But with a simplified tool like I suggest that tracks the successful people and their trading patterns instead of a blindingly large market to analyze. I hope to simplify trading. That is why I want to do this.

How will it work?

Originally it will start out as a simply functioning web service. One that can look up past historical data for companies by going through past companies’ performance data that is freely available. While there are many tools out there to do this already, this will just be a baby step into understanding the problems and solutions that may be encountered in the future. Whilst figuring the historical data side out, it will also in future note the people who sit in the highest positions in the company e.g Chief Technical Officer (CTO) Chief Executive Officer (CEO) Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and mark them alongside the dates of the prior performance data. Thus, users can see the effects the leaders’ strategies and leadership styles had on the performance of the companies whilst they were at the forefront of operations. After enough data is collected to make adequate ratings of executives performance ,it should be easier to correlate which of them are boons to companies and which ones are hinderances . Then my service should be able to provide a decent recommendation on companies or firms to invest in ,based on the data gathered .Although I hope to add the more traditional features to the service further down the line to attract people who have the knowledge to read raw financial data .The revenue to keep the overheads down would be gathered through a balanced amount of advertisement ,but majority through a low-cost subscription based business model. However complicated the background data workings are, I hope to maintain a smooth and clean user interface for ease of use. And to attract people from novice level traders to experienced traders.

What tools and technology will I use?

For the quantitative data I’ll need the use of either a paid data extraction service such as “Scrapinghub” or “Ephesoft” to peruse the documentation in bulk to extract the necessary data to add to comparison matrixes . The other alternative is to pay a data analyst to manually peruse data and turn it into concise and readable spreadsheets .Although as a new idea/new startup ,it would be far cheaper an option to pay a monthly subscription fee for an already made ,purpose-built software that is designed to be used for these sorts of problems. The website itself will be as simple as possible, aesthetically clean. It will be coded using HTML preferably, although after optimization and needs change, so might the web design tools. GitHub for beginning testing might also be a necessity.

What skills will I need?

The skills needed to create the website itself will no doubt include HTML proficiency, potentially CSS too depending on how/what the service grows into with added features. No hardware will be required except a computer/s to write the base HTML/CSS on. It should be quite simple to learn the skills, albeit time consuming. Coding the actual website might be a more difficult matter entirely, potentially taking months to refine. And the cost for using the data scraping utilities will add to the difficulty. Depending on which service /payment plan is chosen.

What is the desired outcome?

If the project is successful and the goals and aims are achieved, then it should provide yet another resource for contrasting valuable data to simplify decision making for traders of all experience levels. If it takes becomes more successful than originally intended, then it could potentially branch of and grow into many combined services that are complicated under the surface but follow the simple and easy to use aesthetic nature of the original idea. Which will hopefully just provide more helpful utilities to all experience levels of traders.
